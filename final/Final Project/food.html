Tahitian Food

Tahitian cuisine includes traditional dishes like poisson cru / Ota i'a (raw fish salad), po'e, coconut milk dishes, and tropical fruits. Cooking traditions are important to families and communities.

Preparing food often involves communal effort, reflecting respect, sharing, and connection among people.

Sample Recipes

Ota I'a - Raw fish marinated in coconut milk and lime juice, the preferred dish of most and considered to be the national dish of Tahiti.

Reference: Tahiti Tourisme

Po'e - Po'e is based on fruit and tapioca flour or corn starch and although banana po'e is the most popular version, you can also find po'e mautini (pumpkin), taro, 'umara (sweet potato), papaya and guava.

Reference: Tahiti Tourisme

Ka'aku - A delicious Marquesan recipe combines the fruit of the breadfruit tree, the uru (called mei in Marquesan), pounded and mixed with coconut milk. A flavorful dish that is highly appreciated in the Marquesas Islands but also in the other islands of French Polynesia.
