Answers

1. The form tag has an "action" attribute. What does it do?

This attribute tells the form WHERE to send informations when you click submit.

2. The form tag has a "method" attribute. What does it do?

This one tells the form HOW to send informations to the website. It usually uses the values "GET" and "POST"

3. What is the difference between a "POST" and a "GET"?

The difference between is that GET puts your information in the website address, while POST hides your information and sends it safely in the background, which is good for sending passwords or big forms.
